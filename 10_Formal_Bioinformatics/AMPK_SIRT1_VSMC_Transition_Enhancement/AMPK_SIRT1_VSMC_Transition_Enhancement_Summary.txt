Interpretation for manuscript:
The substate-level analysis does not support a simple model in which AMPK-SIRT1 activity is highest in contractile VSMCs and lost monotonically during transition. Instead, the existing AMPK-SIRT1 module is modestly higher in inflammatory VSMCs and positively associated with inflammatory/transition-pressure metrics, while ECM-remodeling associations are weaker and directionally mixed. Together with CellChat and TF/regulon results, these findings support a context-dependent AMPK-SIRT1-linked stress/remodeling response during VSMC transition. Therefore, the manuscript should describe AMPK-SIRT1 as a remodeling-associated and externally supported vulnerability/protective axis, not as a substate-proven causal protector of contractile identity.
